Ethical Dilemma Analysis – Alternative Approach

The ethical dilemma in this project involved balancing data collection with participant privacy. Tracking progress was necessary to improve instruction, but excessive data collection could compromise the dignity and trust of elderly participants.

The ethical principles guiding my decision were honesty, responsibility, respect for privacy, and stewardship of information. Rather than collecting detailed personal data, I adopted an alternative approach that focused on anonymized progress metrics and voluntary feedback.

This alternative was ethically superior because it protected participant privacy while still allowing the program to improve. It fostered trust, respected agency, and aligned technical practice with moral responsibility.

IT Project: Refinement and Ethical Integration – Project Refinement Record

Initially, the project functioned as general technical support. However, participant anxiety, limited hardware capabilities, and inconsistent learning outcomes revealed the need for refinement.

To address these challenges, I transitioned the project into a structured instructional design model. Lessons were broken into small, achievable goals to reduce anxiety and build confidence. Accessibility settings were configured for low vision and limited dexterity, and older hardware was optimized for performance.

Ethical considerations were integrated by treating participant data as a form of stewardship. Progress tracking was limited to essential metrics using Excel and Power BI, ensuring transparency without compromising privacy.

These refinements resulted in a measurable increase in participant confidence and engagement. The project became both more technically effective and ethically grounded.
